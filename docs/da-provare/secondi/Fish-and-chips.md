## Ingredienti

### Fish and chips

| Ingredienti | Ingredienti |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| **4 x 200g (7oz)** porzioni di pesce bianco adatto alla frittura (es. nasello, snapper, platessa) | **8** patate grandi adatte per le chips (es. Sebago, Russet Burbank, Maris Piper) |
| spicchi di limone | |

### Salsa tartara

| Ingredienti | Ingredienti |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| **200g (7oz)** maionese | zeste di **1** limone |
| **3** scalogni, tritati finemente | **8** cetriolini sott'aceto, tritati finemente |
| **3 cucchiai (45ml)** capperi baby, tritati grossolanamente | piccolo mazzo di aneto fresco, tritato finemente |

### Frittura e pastella

| Ingredienti | Ingredienti |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| **4 litri (7 pints)** olio per friggere (olio di arachidi o sego) | **200g (7oz)** farina 00, più extra per infarinare |
| **200g (7oz)** farina di riso bianca | **1 cucchiaino** lievito in polvere |
| **1 cucchiaio (15ml)** miele | **300ml (10 fl oz)** vodka |
| **300ml (10 fl oz)** lager o birra chiara | sale e pepe bianco |

## Procedimento

### Preparare le chips

1. Peel the potatoes, rinse thoroughly, and cut into thick chips, about 2cm (¾ inch) square.
2. Place the chips in a bowl of cold water and soak for 10–15 minutes to remove excess starch.
3. Rinse the chips under fresh water, then add them to a large pot. Cover with cold water, add a large pinch of salt, and bring to a simmer.
4. Cook until the chips are soft and nearly falling apart.
5. Remove carefully and place on a wire rack over a tray. Refrigerate until completely cool.

### Preparare la salsa tartara

1. In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, lemon zest, diced shallots, dill, cornichons, and capers.
2. Mix thoroughly, cover, and refrigerate until needed.

### Prima frittura delle chips

> Scaldare l'olio a 140°C (285°F)

1. Fry the cooled chips in batches for 6–7 minutes until the outside is set but not coloured.
2. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels. Set aside to cool.

### Preparare la pastella

1. In a large bowl, whisk together the plain flour, rice flour, and baking powder.
2. Gradually add the lager, vodka, honey, and a pinch of salt, whisking until the batter is smooth and has the consistency of pouring cream.
3. Chill in the fridge until ready to use.

### Cuocere il pesce

> Scaldare l'olio a 180°C (350°F)

1. Lightly season the fish fillets on both sides with salt.
2. Prepare a tray of seasoned flour (mixing salt and white pepper). Coat each fish fillet in the flour, shaking off any excess. Dip the fish into the batter, ensuring it is well coated.
3. Carefully lower the fish into the hot oil, holding it halfway in for a few seconds to help the batter set before fully submerging.
4. Fry the fish for 8–10 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
5. Remove to a wire rack to drain, season with salt, and keep warm in an oven set to 70°C (160°F) if cooking in batches.

### Seconda frittura delle chips

1. With the oil still at 180°C (350°F), fry the chips again in batches for 8–10 minutes until golden and crispy.
2. Drain excess oil and season generously with salt.

### Servire

1. Arrange the fish on scrunched parchment paper for a traditional look.
2. Serve alongside the crispy chips, tartar sauce, and a wedge of lemon.
